## Craft your video interview questions

Xinterview AI gives you three ways to build your interview questions: AI-generated questions, template questions, and custom questions.

- **AI-generated questions** help you create a structured set of questions faster when you want a strong starting point.
- **Template questions** give you a ready-made format that keeps interviews consistent across similar roles.
- **Custom questions** let you write role-specific prompts when you need tighter control over what candidates answer.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose how to create your questions">
    Select the question source that best fits the role and your hiring process.

    Use AI-generated questions when you want help drafting interview content quickly. Use template questions when you want a repeatable format. Use custom questions when the role needs specific prompts, technical detail, or company-specific context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add or review the questions">
    Build the interview set so it matches the position you created in step 1.

    Keep the questions focused on the skills, experience, and responsibilities that matter most for the role. A shorter, targeted set usually gives you clearer candidate responses than a long list of overlapping prompts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Refine the candidate experience">
    Check the order, wording, and scope of the questions before you continue.

    Make sure the interview starts with straightforward questions and moves toward more specific ones. That keeps the session easier to follow and helps candidates answer with confidence.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="info">

If you expect candidates to record on mobile, keep questions concise and easy to answer on camera.

</Callout>

## Configure answer settings

These controls shape how candidates respond to each question.

### Allowed takes

Set how many attempts a candidate gets for each answer.

Use a lower number when you want more decisive responses. Use a higher number when the role benefits from a second try or when you want to reduce pressure during recording.

### Thinking time

Set how long candidates can prepare before recording starts.

Give more thinking time for open-ended or complex questions. Use less time when you want quick, instinctive responses.

### Allocated time

Set the maximum time candidates have to answer each question.

Keep this aligned with the question type. Short time limits work well for focused prompts, while longer limits fit detailed answers or explanation-heavy roles.

## Finish and review

Tailor the questions and answer settings to match the role you are hiring for. Clear question design and balanced timing help candidates give better responses and help you compare answers more easily.
